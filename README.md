## The Problem — Phase 01: Needs Analysis & Data-Driven Thinking

Phishing-related incidents increased **15% in Q3** — despite 100% compliance on mandatory annual security video training.

The data said one thing. The root cause said another.

Using a **Fishbone (Ishikawa) Diagram** and structured stakeholder interviews with the CISO and HR, the investigation surfaced the real culprit: **Compliance Fatigue**. Employees knew the rules — but had never built the *habit* of applying them under high-pressure, real-world conditions. The existing training produced knowledge. It didn't produce behaviour.

**Root causes identified:**
- Training designed for compliance checkboxes, not habit formation
- No feedback loop between training and real-world application
- High workload pressure suppressed knowledge retrieval at the moment of attack
- Zero measurement of behaviour change beyond completion rates

---

## The Strategy — Phase 02: Instructional Design & Curriculum Planning

### Backward Design
Started with the target end behaviour — *proactive reporting of suspicious emails* — then worked backward to identify the knowledge, habits, and motivational triggers needed to produce it.

### SAM Model (Successive Approximation)
A rapid prototype of the "Header Challenge" mini-game was developed and tested with a pilot group before company-wide launch. Iterative feedback shaped the final experience — not a single waterfall build.

### 30-Day Scaffolded Learning Journey

| Week | Phase | Focus |
|------|-------|-------|
| Week 1 | Awareness | Recall — Identify the 5 key indicators of a phishing email header |
| Week 2 | Practice | Apply — Classify simulated emails under time pressure |
| Week 3 | Competition | Analyse — Decompose spoofed metadata across 3 attack types |
| Week 4 | Habit Lock | Evaluate — Proactively report suspicious emails without prompt |

Each phase aligns to **Bloom's Taxonomy** — from Remember → Apply → Analyse → Evaluate — ensuring the learning journey builds toward behavioural habit, not just knowledge recall.

---

## The Build — Phase 03: EdTech Implementation

Three interlocking components, each targeting a specific root cause.

### 🏆 Interactive Gamification
A 30-day tournament where departments earn points for reporting mock-phishing emails. Real-time leaderboards and "Elite Defender" badges drive social motivation and healthy team competition. Addresses: *low motivation* and *no feedback loop*.

### 🥽 AR-Based Just-in-Time Learning
Using a **patented AR approach**, employees scan physical markers around the office to identify hidden security risks — unlocked screens, exposed documents, unattended devices. Bridges the gap between classroom training and real physical environments. Addresses: *habit formation in context*.

### 📱 Micro-Learning Mini-Game: The Header Detective
A daily **2-minute mobile challenge** to identify spoofed email metadata. Fits learning into the natural flow of work — spaced practice without disrupting productivity. Addresses: *cognitive load* and *time pressure at work*.

> The Header Detective is playable as an interactive demo inside the case study site — with live round scoring and feedback.
